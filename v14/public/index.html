STEP-BY-STEP TRANSITION GUIDE

1
Do Nothing Until Legal Matters Are Resolved
Before you change a single thing publicly, confirm the JMW Solicitors / Simon Aylett matter is fully closed and no other claims are live. Publishing the full backstory while proceedings are active creates evidence risk. The rebrand itself is safe — the story video is not.
2
Rename the Channel (Safe to Do Now)
Go to YouTube Studio → Settings → Channel → Basic Info. Change the channel name to "Rock Bottom to Roaming". This is a neutral action that doesn\'t say anything about Co-Lab. YouTube allows name changes. Do this first — it takes 90 days to reflect everywhere so do it early.
3
Update Channel Art & About Section
Replace Co-Lab branding with RBTR branding. Update the About section to: "A family overland expedition. 45 countries. 38,000 miles. Rock Bottom to Roaming — starting 2027. Subscribe to follow the build from day one." No mention of Co-Lab at this stage.
4
Decide What to Do With Old Co-Lab Videos
You have three options: (A) Leave them public — they show your skill and your history. (B) Set them to Unlisted — visible via direct link but not in search or on channel. (C) Delete them — clean slate but loses the build history. Recommendation: Set to Unlisted for now. They protect nothing by being public and they confuse new visitors. You can always restore them later with a "This is where it started" framing.
5
Upload a "Channel Transition" Pinned Post (Community Tab)
Post a simple Community update: "This channel is evolving. I\'m Ben — 20 years custom vehicle builder. I\'m working on something new and I want you here for it. Watch this space." Short. No drama. No explanation. Posts it before the first video to warm the existing audience up.
6
Post a 60-Second Teaser First (Before the Full Video)
Before the full story video, post a 60-second teaser: the Arocs, the workshop, Ben\'s hands, a few words. No narration needed. Let the truck do the talking. Title: "Something is coming. July 2027." This builds intrigue, gets existing subs engaged, and tests the algorithm before the main launch.
7
Launch the Full Story Video
Once all legal proceedings are formally concluded and you have written confirmation — go with the full script. Do not rely on your own assessment that it is "probably fine". Get confirmation from a solicitor that the JMW matter and any other potential claims are closed before the full story video goes live. This is a one-hour legal review and costs £150–300. It is not optional. This is the moment. Everything else is in service of this drop. Blast it across all platforms simultaneously. Email any existing Co-Lab contacts the day it goes live using the Customer Email templates.
8
Pin the Video & Add End Screens to All Old Content
Pin the launch video at the top of your channel. Go back to all Unlisted Co-Lab videos and add an end screen pointing to the RBTR channel. This turns your archive into a funnel for new subscribers.
9
Maintain a Regular Upload Rhythm From Day One
The algorithm rewards consistency harder than quality. One video per week minimum from launch day. It doesn\'t need to be perfect. It needs to be real and consistent. The build content carries itself.

YOUTUBE — HOW TO DOWNLOAD YOUR VIDEOS

1
Download 4K Video Downloader — Free
Go to 4kdownload.com/4k-video-downloader — download and install the free version. Works on Mac and Windows. This is the cleanest, fastest tool for this job with no ads or malware.
2
Download Individual Videos
Open the Co-Lab YouTube channel. Right-click any video → Copy video URL. Open 4K Video Downloader → Paste Link → choose quality (always pick 1080p or 4K original quality) → Download. The file saves to your Downloads folder as an MP4.
3
Download the Entire Channel at Once
In 4K Video Downloader go to File → Paste Link and paste the URL of the Co-Lab channel homepage (not a specific video — the whole channel URL). It will detect all videos and let you download everything in one batch. Select all → Download. This could be a large file depending on how many videos there are — do this on WiFi.
4
Organise Into B-Roll Categories
Once downloaded, create folders on an external SSD: Welding / Fabrication, Paint & Body, Vehicle Reveals, Workshop Atmosphere, Time Lapses, Before & Afters. Tag and move clips into categories. These become your searchable b-roll library in the Media Vault section of this portal.
5
Alternative — YouTube Studio Direct Download
Since you have access to the channel, go to studio.youtube.com → Videos → click the three dots next to any video → Download. YouTube Studio lets you download your own videos directly in original quality. No third-party tool needed. Only works for your own channel videos.

INSTAGRAM — HOW TO DOWNLOAD YOUR POSTS & REELS

1
Download Your Own Data (Everything at Once)
The cleanest method: Instagram → Settings → Accounts Centre → Your information and permissions → Download your information → Request download → choose Videos and photos → select date range → Request. Instagram emails you a link within 24–48 hours to download a ZIP file containing every photo and video you\'ve ever posted in original quality.
2
Download Individual Reels or Posts
For individual videos: open the Reel → tap the three dots → Save to device (if available). If not shown, use the free app Reels Saver or DownloadGram on your phone — paste the post URL and it downloads the original MP4. Again, completely fine for your own content.
3
What to Look For in the Co-Lab Archive
Specifically hunt for: hands-on close-up shots (grinding, welding sparks, paint spraying), vehicle transformation sequences, before and after reveals, workshop atmosphere (wide shots of the space, tools laid out), and any reactions or emotional moments when clients see their finished vehicle. All of this is perfect RBTR b-roll for the launch video and ongoing build content.

1
Import All B-Roll Into a Master Media Pool
Open DaVinci Resolve → New Project: "RBTR B-Roll Library". Media Pool → Import your categorised folders. Resolve imports everything and keeps your folder structure intact as bins. You now have a searchable library of every Co-Lab clip.

Right-click any clip → Clip Attributes → add keywords: "welding", "paint", "reveal", "hands", "workshop". Then create Smart Bins that automatically pull all clips tagged with the same keyword. Search "welding" and every welding clip from 20 years appears instantly.

Speed Ramp for Modern Feel
Co-Lab footage was shot for a different era. In Resolve: right-click clip → Change Clip Speed → use retime curves to create speed ramps (slow to fast). This transforms 2015-era footage into cinematic 2027-looking content instantly. Add a desaturated LUT over the top for the RBTR colour signature.

THE CARNET DE PASSAGE — EXPLAINED

The Carnet de Passage en Douane (CPD) is a passport for your vehicle. It guarantees to customs authorities that you will not sell the vehicle in their country — and if you do, the issuing organisation (RAC in the UK) pays the import duty on your behalf and then chases you for the money.

Without a Carnet, you cannot take the Arocs into Morocco, any of West or East Africa, India, Nepal, or most of Southeast Asia. You would either be turned away at the border or forced to pay import duty equal to 100–400% of the vehicle's value as a cash deposit — refundable only when you leave.

Cost Structure
Admin fee: ~£150–300
Security deposit: up to 4× vehicle value
Deposit options: cash / bank guarantee / AA/RAC bond
Bond typically ~10% of deposit needed as premium
For a £60k truck = up to £240k deposit needed. Get a bond — don't pay cash.

CONTENT OVERRIDE
The System That Bypasses the Perfectionism Loop
Read this first — it's importantYour perfectionism isn't a flaw. It's what makes your work excellent. But for content creation it's working against you because the standard you're applying — professional, polished, perfect from day one — is the wrong standard for where you are right now. This protocol replaces the standard with a process. When you follow the process, the decision about whether it's good enough is already made. You just do the steps.

THE 5-STEP CONTENT OVERRIDE PROTOCOL

1
Set the camera up before you decide what to film
The biggest barrier is starting. So remove the starting decision. When you walk into the workshop, the first thing you do is set up the camera. Not film. Just set it up. Point it at the work. Press record. Now it's running. You're not performing — you're just working with a camera on. This is how the best build content in the world is made. Not scripted. Just documented.
2
The only rule while filming: say out loud what you're doing and why
Not to the camera. To yourself. The way you'd explain it to someone standing next to you. "I'm cutting the RHS at this angle because..." That's it. That's the content. Twenty years of knowledge narrated as you work. Nobody else on YouTube has what you have. No script needed.
3
At the end of the session, film one minute to camera — no notes
One minute. What you did today. What problem you solved. What you found out. No script, no preparation, no notes. Just talk. You're not presenting. You're telling someone what happened. If you stumble, keep going. If you say something wrong, correct it on camera. That's real. Real is what people subscribe to.
4
Post the rough cut before you watch it back
This is the hardest step and the most important one. The reason perfectionism stalls content is the watch-back loop — you film it, you watch it, you hate it, you re-film it, you watch that, you hate it less but still hate it, you don't post. Break the loop. Do a rough cut in CapCut on your phone. Top and tail it. Add a title card. Post it. Do not watch it back first. The audience's reaction tells you what worked. Your own judgment at this stage tells you nothing useful.
5
Read the comments once. Note one thing. Move on.
Don't live in the comments. Read them once, note one piece of useful feedback — one thing the audience responded to, one question they asked, one thing to do differently next time. Write it in the Content Notes below. Then move on to the next piece of content. Improvement happens across episodes, not within them.
